# Fast Mental Math Calculation Tricks

This document covers practical tricks for performing mental math quickly, categorized by the basic arithmetic operations.

## Addition

### 1. Left-to-Right Addition
**Explanation:** Instead of adding from right to left like you would on paper, add from left to right. This keeps the larger, more significant numbers in your head first, making it easier to estimate and remember.
**Worked Example:** `345 + 427`
1. Add the hundreds: `300 + 400 = 700`
2. Add the tens: `40 + 20 = 60` (Running total: `760`)
3. Add the ones: `5 + 7 = 12`
4. Final total: `760 + 12 = 772`

### 2. Making Tens (or Hundreds)
**Explanation:** Round one number to the nearest ten or hundred to make the addition easier, then adjust the result by adding or subtracting the difference.
**Worked Example:** `48 + 37`
1. Round `48` up to `50` (added `2`).
2. Add the `50` to `37`: `50 + 37 = 87`
3. Subtract the `2` you added earlier: `87 - 2 = 85`

## Subtraction

### 1. The "Round Up and Adjust" Method (Complement Method)
**Explanation:** When subtracting a number close to a multiple of 10 or 100, round the number you are subtracting up to that multiple, subtract it, and then add back the difference.
**Worked Example:** `73 - 28`
1. Round `28` up to `30` (added `2`).
2. Subtract `30` from `73`: `73 - 30 = 43`
3. Add back the `2`: `43 + 2 = 45`

### 2. Subtracting from 1,000 (All from 9 and the last from 10)
**Explanation:** A quick way to subtract any number from 1,000 (or 10,000, 100,000, etc.) is to subtract every digit from 9, except the last digit, which you subtract from 10.
**Worked Example:** `1,000 - 463`
1. Subtract the hundreds from 9: `9 - 4 = 5`
2. Subtract the tens from 9: `9 - 6 = 3`
3. Subtract the ones from 10: `10 - 3 = 7`
4. Answer: `537`

## Multiplication

### 1. Multiplying by 5
**Explanation:** Since 5 is half of 10, to multiply any number by 5, multiply it by 10 (add a zero at the end) and then divide it by 2.
**Worked Example:** `48 × 5`
1. Multiply by 10: `48 × 10 = 480`
2. Divide by 2: `480 / 2 = 240`

### 2. Multiplying by 11
**Explanation:** For a two-digit number, write down the first digit, add the two digits together and put the result in the middle, and then write down the last digit. (If the middle sum is 10 or more, carry the 1 over to the first digit).
**Worked Example:** `35 × 11`
1. First digit: `3`
2. Middle digit: `3 + 5 = 8`
3. Last digit: `5`
4. Answer: `385`

*(Carry over example: `75 × 11` -> `7`, `7+5=12`, `5` -> `(7+1)25` -> `825`)*

## Division

### 1. Dividing by 5
**Explanation:** Dividing by 5 is the opposite of multiplying by 5. Multiply the number by 2 and then divide by 10 (move the decimal point one place to the left).
**Worked Example:** `235 / 5`
1. Multiply by 2: `235 × 2 = 470`
2. Divide by 10: `470 / 10 = 47`

### 2. Dividing by 4
**Explanation:** To divide by 4, simply halve the number, and then halve it again.
**Worked Example:** `124 / 4`
1. Halve the number: `124 / 2 = 62`
2. Halve it again: `62 / 2 = 31`
